Hypertension (high blood pressure) is a chronic medical condition in which the blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated. 
Symptoms may include headaches, shortness of breath, or nosebleeds, though it often has no warning signs.
Risk factors: age, genetics, high salt intake, obesity, sedentary lifestyle.
Management includes lifestyle modifications (diet, exercise) and medications like ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers.
